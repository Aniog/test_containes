# SSourcing China Design System

## Brand direction
SSourcing China should feel clean, trustworthy, practical, and international. The design should support B2B lead generation for overseas buyers who need supplier verification, quality control, production follow-up, and shipping coordination in China.

## Typography
- Primary font: Inter, loaded from Google Fonts in `index.html`.
- Use confident, readable headings with tight line height: `text-4xl md:text-6xl font-semibold tracking-tight`.
- Body text should be calm and practical: `text-base md:text-lg leading-7`.
- Use uppercase micro-labels sparingly for section labels: `text-xs font-semibold uppercase tracking-[0.2em]`.

## Colors
Use Tailwind utility classes and semantic colors rather than arbitrary inline styles.
- Page background: warm slate/white surfaces, e.g. `bg-slate-50`, `bg-white`.
- Primary text: `text-slate-950`.
- Secondary text: `text-slate-600` and `text-slate-700`.
- Primary brand: deep blue, e.g. `bg-blue-700`, `text-blue-700`, `border-blue-200`.
- Accent: amber for operational highlights, e.g. `bg-amber-400`, `text-amber-700`, `border-amber-200`.
- Dark sections: `bg-slate-950` with `text-white` and `text-slate-300`.

## Layout and spacing
- Use a centered max-width container: `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-4 sm:px-6 lg:px-8`.
- Section spacing: `py-16 md:py-24`.
- Cards: `rounded-3xl border border-slate-200 bg-white shadow-sm`.
- Keep desktop layouts spacious with multi-column grids; stack on mobile.

## Visual style
- Use realistic factory, QC, warehouse, shipping, and business coordination visuals through the Strikingly stock image data attributes.
- Prefer subtle shadows, fine borders, and blue/amber accents.
- Avoid exaggerated claims, flashy gradients, or consumer-style language.

## CTAs
- Primary CTA: solid blue button with white text, `bg-blue-700 text-white hover:bg-blue-800`.
- Secondary CTA: white/slate button with border, `border-slate-300 bg-white text-slate-900 hover:bg-slate-50`.
- CTA copy should be direct: “Get a Free Sourcing Quote”.

## Do's
- Make every value and label clearly readable.
- Use practical B2B wording and specific operational benefits.
- Keep forms simple, with visible labels and helpful placeholder text.
- Use trust markers such as verification, inspection, production tracking, and shipping coordination.

## Don'ts
- Do not use unrealistic claims such as guaranteed lowest price or risk-free sourcing.
- Do not use low-contrast text on images or colored backgrounds.
- Do not overload pages with decorative visuals that distract from the inquiry goal.
