# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, feminine, premium, and calm. The site should feel elevated without looking flashy, discount-led, or trend-chasing.

## Core palette
Use one consistent direction built around rich espresso surfaces, soft ivory content areas, and restrained champagne-gold accents.

- `bg-velmora-ink`: deep espresso-plum for hero, footer, and premium surfaces. Example: `bg-velmora-ink`
- `bg-velmora-ivory`: warm ivory for the main page background. Example: `bg-velmora-ivory`
- `bg-velmora-panel`: slightly darker ivory for cards and panels. Example: `bg-velmora-panel`
- `text-velmora-ink`: near-black brown for primary content on light surfaces. Example: `text-velmora-ink`
- `text-velmora-muted`: softened taupe for supporting copy. Example: `text-velmora-muted`
- `text-velmora-gold`: champagne-gold accent for highlights and micro-details. Example: `text-velmora-gold`
- `border-velmora-line`: translucent warm divider for hairlines. Example: `border-velmora-line`
- `bg-velmora-accent`: muted gold button and badge accent. Example: `bg-velmora-accent`
- `text-velmora-accent-foreground`: dark espresso text on gold accents. Example: `text-velmora-accent-foreground`

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Playfair Display, elegant, editorial, refined.
- Body and UI: Inter.
- Product names should appear uppercase with wide tracking. Example: `uppercase tracking-[0.28em]`
- Large headlines should use airy line-height and generous spacing.

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous whitespace, especially between homepage sections.
- Keep content in refined containers such as `max-w-7xl` with wide horizontal padding.
- Use thin borders and hairline dividers rather than heavy boxes.
- Avoid cramped card layouts on desktop.

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium, with either solid muted-gold fills or clean outlined ivory treatments.
- Cards should use soft shadows, subtle border lines, and restrained hover movement.
- Sticky navigation should begin transparent over the hero and transition to a solid, slightly blurred background on scroll.
- Drawers and overlays should use dark translucent backdrops and elegant panel spacing.

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit, editorial jewelry imagery.
- Favor close crops, dark neutral backdrops, soft skin tones, and product-on-model compositions.
- Avoid busy, noisy, or discount-style lifestyle images.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and readable.
- Use restrained hover transitions.
- Maintain editorial rhythm with asymmetry where useful.
- Make mobile layouts feel luxurious, not compressed.

## Don’t
- Do not use bright promotional colors.
- Do not use heavy gradients or glossy effects.
- Do not use low-contrast text on ivory or gold surfaces.
- Do not make buttons or badges look like sale stickers.
