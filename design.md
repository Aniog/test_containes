# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand Direction
Velmora Fine Jewelry uses a quiet-luxury editorial aesthetic: warm, refined, spacious, and premium without feeling flashy or discount-driven.

## Color Palette
Use this palette consistently across the site.

- `velmora-ink` — deep espresso-plum base for headers, footer, and dark editorial surfaces
- `velmora-ivory` — warm ivory background for the main site canvas
- `velmora-panel` — soft champagne panel background for cards and elevated sections
- `velmora-gold` — muted metallic accent for buttons, dividers, icons, and highlights
- `velmora-mist` — subtle neutral border and divider tone
- `velmora-muted` — secondary body text on light surfaces
- `velmora-soft` — secondary body text on dark surfaces

## Typography
- Headings, logo, product names: `Playfair Display`, elegant and editorial
- Body, navigation, controls, pricing, labels: `Inter`
- Product names should appear in uppercase with generous tracking, for example `tracking-[0.28em]` only if there is no existing named tracking utility available. Prefer `tracking-[0.24em]` to `tracking-[0.32em]` for readability.

## Layout & Spacing
- Favor generous whitespace and calm section rhythm.
- Use large imagery and clean blocks rather than dense content.
- Max content width should feel premium and breathable, for example `max-w-7xl` with generous horizontal padding.
- Use thin dividers and restrained shadows.

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: either solid `velmora-gold` with dark text or transparent/ivory with subtle border.
- Cards should use warm light surfaces with readable dark text.
- Dark sections must explicitly use light foreground text.
- Use rounded corners sparingly; prefer `rounded-2xl` or `rounded-3xl` for editorial modules, smaller radius for controls.
- Hover states should be subtle: opacity, translate-y, soft shadow, or image scale.

## Imagery
- Prefer warm-lit close-ups of gold jewelry, elegant portrait crops, and refined neutral or dark backdrops.
- UGC/reel imagery should feel polished and aspirational, not chaotic.
- Avoid bright pop colors, discount badges, or noisy collage layouts.

## Do
- Keep text highly legible on every surface.
- Use explicit foreground colors for all cards, forms, badges, and dark sections.
- Maintain a premium but accessible feel.
- Ensure mobile layouts remain airy and uncluttered.

## Don’t
- Do not use loud sale styling, bright red discount treatments, or generic marketplace layouts.
- Do not mix multiple accent colors.
- Do not place low-contrast gold text on light gold backgrounds.
- Do not overcrowd desktop layouts or make desktop feel like stacked mobile cards.
