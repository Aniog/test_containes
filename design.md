# Velmora Fine Jewelry Visual System

Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm parchment surfaces, refined deep cocoa contrast, restrained champagne-gold accents, and generous whitespace. The design should feel premium and intimate rather than promotional or loud.

## Palette
- Base surface: warm ivory/parchment (`bg-parchment`, `bg-ivory`) for soft editorial space.
- Deep contrast: cocoa/espresso (`bg-cocoa`, `text-cocoa`, `text-espresso`) for navigation, footer, and readable copy.
- Accent: champagne and antique gold (`bg-champagne`, `text-antique`, `border-champagne`) for CTAs, fine dividers, ratings, and jewelry-inspired details.
- Muted neutrals: stone, mist, and linen (`text-stone`, `bg-linen`, `border-mist`) for secondary text, cards, and hairline rules.

## Typography
- Headings and product names use Cormorant Garamond: elegant serif, editorial, high contrast.
- UI and body copy use Inter: clean, modern, accessible.
- Product names should be uppercase with wide tracking (`uppercase tracking-product`) and a refined serif style.

## Layout and spacing
- Mobile-first with generous vertical rhythm and clean stacked sections.
- Desktop layouts use editorial split sections, multi-column grids, and broad margins.
- Use thin hairline borders (`border border-mist` or `border-champagne/40`) instead of heavy outlines.
- Avoid crowded product cards; keep imagery dominant and text minimal.

## Components
- Buttons: premium solid cocoa or champagne styles with subtle hover transitions. Avoid bright sale-like colors.
- Cards: warm ivory/linen surfaces, soft shadows, and restrained borders.
- Navigation: transparent over hero, solid parchment/cocoa text after scroll.
- Imagery: warm-lit gold jewelry, model closeups, neutral or deep backgrounds.

## Do's
- Keep contrast strong and text readable on every surface.
- Use restrained gold accents sparingly.
- Let whitespace and typography signal luxury.
- Favor soft transitions: opacity, translate, subtle shadow.

## Don'ts
- Do not use loud discount colors, badges, or aggressive promotional layouts.
- Do not use generic blue links or saturated e-commerce UI colors.
- Do not place low-contrast beige text on pale surfaces or dark brown text on dark imagery without overlays.
