# ARTITECT MACHINERY Design System

## Brand direction
ARTITECT MACHINERY should feel elegant, engineered, precise, and user friendly. The design should combine premium industrial confidence with clear product communication.

## Color palette
- Primary surface: deep graphite and near-black industrial backgrounds (`bg-slate-950`, `bg-slate-900`).
- Main text on dark surfaces: crisp white and cool slate (`text-white`, `text-slate-200`).
- Light sections: clean zinc/stone whites (`bg-stone-50`, `bg-white`) with dark readable text (`text-slate-950`).
- Accent: warm amber/copper for calls to action and highlights (`bg-amber-500`, `text-amber-500`, `border-amber-400`).
- Support tones: slate and zinc borders (`border-slate-200`, `border-white/10`).

## Typography
- Use Inter as the main font for a modern technical look.
- Headlines should be bold, well-spaced, and concise (`text-4xl md:text-6xl font-semibold tracking-tight`).
- Body copy should be readable and calm (`text-base md:text-lg leading-7`).
- Labels and specifications can use uppercase tracking (`uppercase tracking-[0.18em] text-xs font-semibold`).

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous section spacing (`py-16 md:py-24`) and centered content containers (`max-w-7xl mx-auto px-6`).
- Desktop layouts should use two columns or grids when appropriate; mobile should stack cleanly.
- Cards should have clear padding, rounded corners, and readable foreground colors (`rounded-3xl p-6 md:p-8`).

## Visual style
- Prefer subtle gradients, soft shadows, and thin borders over heavy decoration.
- Industrial product imagery should be used sparingly in hero and product cards with Strikingly stock image tags.
- Use simple line icons to communicate benefits.

## Do's
- Keep all text high contrast and easy to read.
- Use clear CTAs such as “Explore Machines” and “Request a Quote”.
- Make product categories easy to scan.
- Keep the tone confident, technical, and approachable.

## Don'ts
- Do not use low-contrast gray text on dark backgrounds.
- Do not use bright playful colors that weaken the industrial premium feel.
- Do not crowd desktop layouts with mobile-style single-column sections.
