# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand Direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and premium without feeling intimidating. The storefront must highlight gold jewelry with a rich, cinematic backdrop and generous whitespace.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond
- Body and UI: Inter
- Product names should appear in uppercase with wide tracking.

## Color System
Add these named colors to Tailwind config and use them consistently.
- `velmora-ink`: deep espresso-black for hero backgrounds and high-contrast surfaces
- `velmora-noir`: slightly softer dark brown for cards, drawer, footer, and overlays
- `velmora-stone`: warm taupe for supporting panels
- `velmora-sand`: parchment neutral for page backgrounds
- `velmora-ivory`: soft ivory for elevated surfaces
- `velmora-gold`: champagne gold accent for buttons, borders, and highlights
- `velmora-rose`: muted blush accent for editorial moments

## Layout & Spacing
- Use large vertical spacing between homepage sections.
- Keep content in wide containers with breathing room on desktop and comfortable padding on mobile.
- Use thin hairline dividers and soft shadows rather than heavy borders.

## Buttons
- Primary buttons: solid velmora-gold background with dark text.
- Secondary buttons: transparent or ivory with thin gold/stone borders.
- Hover states should be subtle and polished, never bouncy or flashy.

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit, editorial, close-up jewelry imagery.
- Favor deep neutral or parchment backdrops that flatter gold tones.
- UGC cards should mimic elegant social reels rather than casual discount-store content.

## Do
- Use explicit readable text colors on all sections.
- Maintain premium whitespace and restrained motion.
- Keep product cards clean with minimal copy and clear pricing.

## Don't
- Do not use bright sale colors, loud gradients, or bargain-style badges.
- Do not use low-contrast text.
- Do not overcrowd mobile layouts.
