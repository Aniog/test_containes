# SSourcing China Visual Style

## Brand personality
Clean, trustworthy, international, and practical B2B. The website should feel like a reliable sourcing partner for overseas buyers evaluating China suppliers, factory audits, inspections, production follow-up, and shipping coordination.

## Colors
- Primary navy: `brand-navy` for headings, navigation, and serious B2B surfaces. Example Tailwind: `text-brand-navy`, `bg-brand-navy`.
- Industrial blue: `brand-blue` for primary CTAs and links. Example Tailwind: `bg-brand-blue`, `text-brand-blue`.
- Steel blue: `brand-steel` for secondary accents and service icons.
- Warm amber: `brand-amber` for highlights, badges, and trust markers.
- Light background: `brand-surface` and `brand-muted` for page and card backgrounds.
- Text: `brand-ink` for readable body text and `brand-subtle` for secondary copy.

## Typography
Use Inter from Google Fonts for a modern, international B2B feel. Headings are bold and compact; body text is clear, practical, and easy to scan.

## Layout and spacing
Use generous whitespace, strong alignment, and a maximum content width around `max-w-7xl`. Desktop layouts should use two or three columns where useful; mobile layouts should stack cleanly with `md:` and `lg:` responsive breakpoints.

## Visual style
Cards should use light surfaces, subtle borders, and soft shadows. Use realistic factory, QC, warehouse, product, and shipping visuals via the Strikingly stock image attributes. Avoid decorative clutter.

## Do
- Keep CTA buttons prominent and consistent.
- Use explicit readable text colors on every custom surface.
- Use concise, practical copy that helps overseas buyers understand the process.
- Use trust signals like documentation, audit checks, inspection reports, and shipment coordination.

## Don't
- Do not use exaggerated claims like guaranteed lowest price or risk-free sourcing.
- Do not use low-contrast text.
- Do not rely on generic consumer-style gradients or playful colors.
