# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and premium without looking flashy or promotional.

## Color system
Use one consistent palette built from Tailwind default colors:
- Primary backdrop: `stone-950`, `stone-900`
- Warm surface tones: `stone-50`, `stone-100`, `stone-200`
- Premium accent: `amber-600`, `amber-500`
- Soft supporting highlight: `rose-100`
- Body copy: `stone-700`, `stone-600`
- Hairlines and dividers: `stone-200`, `white/10`, `white/15`

## Typography
- Display / editorial headings: Cormorant Garamond
- Body / UI: Inter
- Product names: uppercase with wide tracking and serif styling

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous whitespace with strong vertical rhythm
- Prefer max widths such as `max-w-7xl`, `max-w-6xl`, `max-w-5xl`
- Keep sections airy with `py-16`, `py-20`, `py-24` depending on emphasis
- Use thin dividers and restrained shadows instead of heavy borders

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: dark solid, warm accent solid, or thin outlined
- Cards should use soft surfaces like `bg-stone-50` or deep editorial surfaces like `bg-stone-900`
- Hover states should be subtle: slight opacity, translate, shadow, or image scale
- Product cards should never feel discount-oriented or crowded

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit closeups of gold jewelry on skin, fabric, stone, or velvet
- Prefer editorial compositions over flat catalog shots
- Keep backgrounds neutral, dark, or creamy to flatter gold tones

## Do
- Use strong text contrast on every section
- Keep the site calm and elegant
- Make mobile layouts breathable and touch-friendly
- Let imagery and typography carry the premium tone

## Do not
- Do not use bright promotional colors
- Do not use heavy gradients or loud badges
- Do not crowd cards with excessive labels
- Do not use low-contrast text on warm surfaces
