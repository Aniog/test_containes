# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Direction
Quiet luxury with a warm editorial point of view. The storefront should feel intimate, polished, and premium-but-accessible: generous whitespace, refined typography, restrained motion, and gold-friendly neutral surfaces.

## Color Palette
Use these named Tailwind colors only for brand surfaces and accents:

- `velmora-ivory` `#F8F3EA` — primary page background and warm negative space.
- `velmora-pearl` `#EEE5D8` — alternate panels, soft cards, trust strips.
- `velmora-linen` `#D8C9B6` — fine borders, subdued dividers.
- `velmora-champagne` `#C7A46C` — primary metallic accent, CTA backgrounds, rating stars.
- `velmora-antique` `#9B7339` — darker gold accent for hover and readable small details.
- `velmora-cocoa` `#4C382D` — secondary text on light surfaces.
- `velmora-umber` `#2D211B` — primary text on light surfaces.
- `velmora-obsidian` `#18110E` — deep editorial sections and nav overlays.
- `velmora-rose` `#B98778` — subtle warm emphasis for badges or editorial captions.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond, elegant serif, high contrast, editorial scale.
- Body and UI: Manrope, clean sans-serif, legible at small sizes.
- Product names should be uppercase with wide letter spacing.
- Navigation and microcopy should use small uppercase tracking.

## Layout & Spacing
- Mobile-first, but desktop should use editorial multi-column layouts.
- Prefer generous section padding (`py-16`, `md:py-24`) and centered containers (`max-w-7xl`).
- Use thin hairline dividers (`border-velmora-linen`) and restrained shadows.
- Full-bleed hero imagery should be warm, model-focused, and softly overlaid for text readability.

## Components
- Buttons: solid champagne with dark text for primary actions, outlined hairline for secondary actions.
- Cards: warm ivory/pearl surfaces, subtle border, image-forward with refined hover states.
- Cart drawer and menus: ivory surfaces with explicit dark text for readability.
- Forms: warm neutral fields, thin borders, clear focus rings using champagne.

## Do's
- Keep visual rhythm spacious and calm.
- Use warm gold/neutral imagery queries for jewelry closeups and model styling.
- Make all text clearly readable against its background.
- Use subtle transitions (`duration-300`, `ease-out`) rather than flashy animation.

## Don'ts
- Do not use discount-style bright colors or aggressive sale badges.
- Do not overcrowd product grids.
- Do not use low-contrast muted text on pearl/ivory surfaces.
- Do not introduce additional brand colors outside the palette.
