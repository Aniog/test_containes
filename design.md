# SSourcing China Design Guide

## Brand Direction
- Clean, trustworthy, practical B2B presentation for overseas buyers working with a China-based sourcing team.
- International tone: clear English, low hype, operational confidence, and practical explanations.
- Visual mood: modern logistics, factory verification, quality inspection, supplier meetings, packaging, shipping coordination.

## Typography
- Primary font: Inter
- Headings: `font-semibold` to `font-bold`, tracking slightly tight, clear hierarchy
- Body: `text-sm md:text-base leading-7`
- Use calm, readable type. Avoid oversized marketing headlines outside hero sections.

## Color System
- Page background: `bg-slate-50`
- Primary surface: `bg-white`
- Dark sections: `bg-slate-950`
- Primary text: `text-slate-900`
- Secondary text: `text-slate-600`
- Inverse text: `text-white` or `text-slate-100`
- Brand accents: `bg-blue-700`, `text-blue-700`, `border-blue-200`
- Supporting accents: `bg-amber-100`, `text-amber-800`, `border-amber-200`
- Success/trust accents: `bg-emerald-50`, `text-emerald-800`, `border-emerald-200`

## Layout
- Use centered containers: `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-4 sm:px-6 lg:px-8`
- Standard vertical rhythm: `py-16 md:py-24`
- Card spacing: `rounded-2xl border border-slate-200 bg-white p-6 md:p-8`
- Large panels: `rounded-3xl border border-slate-200 bg-white shadow-sm`

## Components
- Buttons: primary `bg-blue-700 text-white hover:bg-blue-800`, secondary `border border-slate-300 bg-white text-slate-900 hover:bg-slate-100`
- Section labels: `inline-flex rounded-full bg-blue-100 px-3 py-1 text-xs font-semibold uppercase tracking-[0.2em] text-blue-800`
- Trust badges: subtle borders, no loud gradients
- Forms: white inputs with `border-slate-300`, strong focus rings in blue, clear labels above fields

## Imagery
- Prefer realistic business visuals: supplier meetings, factory floors, inspection checklists, cartons, pallets, container loading, product sampling.
- Use stock images sparingly and only to support the content.
- Hero imagery should feel operational and international, not generic startup imagery.

## Do
- Keep all text highly legible against its background.
- Use generous whitespace and balanced multi-column desktop layouts.
- Write practical headings and short supporting paragraphs.
- Make CTAs visible without feeling aggressive.

## Don’t
- Do not use neon colors, loud gradients, or exaggerated stats.
- Do not use low-contrast gray-on-gray text.
- Do not stack all desktop content into a narrow single column.
- Do not make claims that sound guaranteed or unrealistic.
