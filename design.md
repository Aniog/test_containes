# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury with a warm editorial edge. The site should feel intimate, elevated, and polished rather than loud, trendy, or promotional. Gold jewelry should always feel luminous against soft neutral surfaces and deep espresso anchors.

## Palette
Use this palette consistently sitewide.

- `velmora-ink` — `#1d1715` — primary text, rich dark surfaces
- `velmora-espresso` — `#342927` — secondary dark backgrounds, footer, cards on dark sections
- `velmora-paper` — `#f7f1e8` — main page background
- `velmora-shell` — `#efe5d8` — secondary surface blocks, trust strips, filter panels
- `velmora-gold` — `#b89153` — primary accent, premium buttons, highlights
- `velmora-gold-deep` — `#8f6d37` — hover and pressed accent state
- `velmora-mist` — `#cab9a2` — borders, hairlines, subtle fills
- `velmora-taupe` — `#746252` — muted readable body text on light surfaces

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`, elegant and airy
- Body, navigation, controls: `Inter`
- Product names should appear in uppercase with generous tracking
- Headings should feel editorial and spacious, never cramped

## Spacing and layout
- Generous padding on every section
- Wide breathing room around imagery and headlines
- Use thin dividers and soft shadows instead of heavy borders
- Mobile-first layout, but preserve multi-column editorial structure on desktop

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: solid gold accent or refined outline
- Cards should use soft surface color, subtle shadow, and clean hierarchy
- Filter panels, drawers, and footers must use explicit foreground colors for readability
- Product imagery should feel warm, close, and editorial

## Motion
- Use restrained transitions only
- Prefer soft fade, lift, and opacity changes
- Avoid flashy animations, bounces, or discount-style attention grabbing

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and text clearly legible
- Use hairline borders in `velmora-mist`
- Let product imagery and whitespace create the luxury feel
- Keep promotional language sparse and tasteful

## Don’t
- Do not use bright sale colors or loud gradients
- Do not stack everything into a single narrow column on desktop
- Do not use low-contrast beige-on-beige text
- Do not make buttons feel overly techy or generic marketplace
