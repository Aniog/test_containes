# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and premium without feeling exclusive or cold. The storefront should resemble a modern jewelry campaign mixed with a clean direct-to-consumer shopping flow.

## Palette
Use one consistent palette sitewide.

- Background ink: deep espresso-brown for high-contrast hero and footer surfaces. Tailwind intent: `bg-stone-950` / custom brand token `brand-ink`.
- Main canvas: warm ivory / soft parchment for the majority of the page. Tailwind intent: `bg-stone-50` / `bg-neutral-50`.
- Secondary surface: soft almond / champagne mist for cards and editorial panels. Tailwind intent: `bg-stone-100`.
- Accent metallic: muted antique gold for buttons, dividers, icons, ratings, and focus accents. Tailwind intent: custom token `brand-gold`.
- Text primary on light: deep charcoal-brown. Tailwind intent: `text-stone-900`.
- Text secondary on light: softened stone. Tailwind intent: `text-stone-600`.
- Text on dark: warm ivory. Tailwind intent: `text-stone-50`.

## Typography
- Headings, product names, logo: `Cormorant Garamond`.
- Body, UI, filters, labels, buttons: `Inter`.
- Product names must be uppercase with noticeable tracking. Tailwind intent: `uppercase tracking-[0.28em]`.
- Section overlines and micro-labels should use small uppercase Inter with wide tracking.

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous whitespace with strong vertical rhythm.
- Default content container should feel editorial, centered, and breathable. Tailwind intent: `mx-auto max-w-7xl px-5 sm:px-6 lg:px-8`.
- Prefer thin hairline dividers. Tailwind intent: `border-stone-200/70` on light surfaces, `border-white/15` on dark surfaces.
- Cards should use soft shadows and low-contrast borders, never heavy drop shadows.

## Imagery
- Large, warm-lit jewelry photography.
- Use dark neutral, taupe, and skin-tone-friendly backgrounds that flatter gold jewelry.
- Product imagery should feel elevated and minimal, not marketplace-like.
- UGC/reels imagery should look candid but still polished.

## Buttons and interactions
- Primary button: dark ink or muted gold fill with warm ivory text.
- Secondary button: transparent or ivory with subtle border.
- Hover effects should be subtle: slight translate, shadow, opacity, or image crossfade.
- Avoid flashy gradients or high-saturation promotional styling.

## Do
- Keep navigation restrained and premium.
- Use serif headlines large and airy.
- Maintain readable text contrast at all times.
- Keep product cards clean and image-forward.
- Make mobile layouts elegant, not cramped.

## Do not
- Do not use loud sale colors, bright reds, or discount retail cues.
- Do not use heavy borders, aggressive shadows, or oversized badges.
- Do not stack everything into a single narrow mobile-style layout on desktop.
- Do not leave important text colors implicit on tinted backgrounds.
