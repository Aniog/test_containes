# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, intimate, and premium without becoming flashy or cold. The experience should flatter gold jewelry and feel giftable, feminine, and elevated.

## Palette
Use one consistent direction sitewide: deep espresso for contrast, warm parchment for surfaces, and brushed bronze for accents.

### Named color tokens
- `velmora-ink`: deep espresso for primary text and dark sections
- `velmora-cacao`: softened dark brown for elevated surfaces
- `velmora-bronze`: warm metallic accent for buttons, dividers, and badges
- `velmora-champagne`: lighter metallic accent for subtle overlays and icons
- `velmora-parchment`: primary page background
- `velmora-sand`: secondary warm neutral surface
- `velmora-mist`: subdued text and borders
- `velmora-rose`: restrained editorial accent

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`
- Body and UI: `Inter`
- Product names: uppercase with wide tracking

## Layout principles
- Generous whitespace, large image-led sections, strong rhythm between sections
- Thin hairline dividers and restrained borders
- Rounded corners should feel soft and premium, not playful
- Subtle hover transitions and soft shadows only
- Mobile-first layout, but desktop should feel spacious and editorial

## Recommended Tailwind usage
- Page background: `bg-velmora-parchment`
- Dark surfaces: `bg-velmora-ink` or `bg-velmora-cacao`
- Primary text: `text-velmora-ink`
- Secondary text: `text-velmora-mist`
- Accent actions: `bg-velmora-ink text-velmora-parchment` or `border border-velmora-bronze`
- Dividers: `border-velmora-mist/30`
- Premium cards: `rounded-3xl bg-velmora-sand shadow-card`

## Do
- Keep surfaces warm and readable
- Use serif type for emotional emphasis and product storytelling
- Let product photography breathe
- Keep interactions smooth and understated
- Use bronze only as an accent, not as the main reading color

## Don’t
- Do not use bright sale-style colors
- Do not use stark pure black and pure white combinations
- Do not create discount-store badges or loud promotional styling
- Do not use overly dense grids without breathing room
- Do not leave important text on inherited colors
