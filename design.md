# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury for demi-fine jewelry: warm, editorial, composed, and premium without feeling exclusive or loud.

## Palette
Use the built-in Tailwind palette consistently.
- Base background: `stone-50`
- Soft surfaces: `stone-100`
- Primary text: `stone-950`
- Secondary text: `stone-600`
- Hairlines / dividers: `stone-200`
- Rich dark sections: `stone-950`
- Warm metallic accent: `amber-600`
- Soft metallic tint: `amber-100`
- Accent hover: `amber-500`

## Typography
- Display / editorial headings: Cormorant Garamond
- Body / UI copy: Inter
- Product names: uppercase with generous tracking using the dedicated `.product-name` utility
- Logo: uppercase serif wordmark using the dedicated `.logo-mark` utility

## Layout and spacing
- Favor generous whitespace and long vertical rhythm
- Use `container-shell` for page width consistency
- Use `section-shell` for section spacing
- Use thin dividers rather than heavy borders
- Prefer soft rounded corners and restrained shadows

## Components
- Primary buttons: dark base with warm accent hover
- Secondary buttons: outline or soft neutral fill
- Cards: warm neutral surface, soft shadow, smooth hover lift
- Inputs: refined borders, clear focus ring, generous padding

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit editorial jewelry imagery
- Favor close-up model shots, silk or stone surfaces, and premium macro details
- Avoid flat generic ecommerce visuals or discount-style badge treatments

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and text clearly readable on every surface
- Let product imagery and typography carry the luxury feeling
- Use amber as a restrained metallic accent, not a loud highlight

## Don't
- Do not use bright sale colors, heavy gradients, or cluttered layouts
- Do not use overly playful iconography
- Do not create dense mobile layouts; keep breathing room even on small screens
