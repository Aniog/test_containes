# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand Direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and intimate. The experience should feel premium and giftable without looking flashy, trend-chasing, or discount-driven.

## Color Palette
Use one consistent warm editorial palette inspired by gold jewelry in candlelit interiors.

- `velmora-pearl` — soft pearl background for the primary canvas
- `velmora-mist` — lighter surface for layered sections and cards
- `velmora-ivory` — warm elevated surface for premium blocks
- `velmora-line` — hairline dividers and subtle borders
- `velmora-sand` — soft supporting neutral for badges and quiet fills
- `velmora-taupe` — muted secondary text
- `velmora-gold` — restrained metallic accent for CTAs and highlights
- `velmora-espresso` — main dark base for text, overlays, and footer
- `velmora-ink` — default readable body text on light backgrounds

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`
- Body copy, navigation, controls: `Inter`
- Product names must be uppercase with wide tracking.
- Eyebrow labels should feel editorial and airy.

## Layout + Spacing
- Generous whitespace, especially around hero, product grids, and storytelling sections.
- Use thin hairline borders and soft shadows.
- Keep desktop layouts spacious and balanced; do not collapse into narrow mobile-style stacks on large screens.
- Mobile should remain elegant with comfortable padding and clear tap targets.

## UI Treatments
- Buttons should feel polished: either solid gold accent with dark text or subtle outlined variants.
- Cards should use warm neutral surfaces with readable dark text.
- Hover states should be soft, never flashy.
- Product and editorial imagery should feel warm-lit, close, and elevated.

## Do
- Use strong contrast for all text and controls.
- Keep sections calm and curated.
- Use serif headlines as focal moments.
- Let gold accents stay restrained.

## Don't
- Do not use bright sale colors, loud gradients, or coupon-style UI.
- Do not use generic marketplace card styling.
- Do not use low-contrast beige-on-beige text.
- Do not overcrowd mobile layouts.
