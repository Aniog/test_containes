# Velmora Fine Jewelry Visual System

Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm ivory surfaces, deep espresso contrast, aged-gold accents, soft champagne panels, and generous whitespace. The storefront should feel premium, calm, and tactile rather than promotional.

## Palette
- Ink: deep espresso for primary text and dark surfaces. Tailwind example: `text-velmora-ink`, `bg-velmora-ink`.
- Ivory: warm page background. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-ivory`.
- Porcelain: elevated card and form surfaces. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-porcelain`.
- Champagne: soft editorial highlight panels. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-champagne`.
- Gold: restrained metallic accent for buttons, rules, icons, and focus states. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-gold`, `text-velmora-gold`.
- Bronze: hover and active accent state. Tailwind example: `hover:bg-velmora-bronze`.
- Taupe: readable secondary text and hairline borders. Tailwind example: `text-velmora-taupe`, `border-velmora-line`.

## Typography
- Headings and product names use Cormorant Garamond for a refined editorial feeling.
- Body, UI labels, filters, prices, and navigation use Inter.
- Product names are uppercase with wide tracking: `uppercase tracking-[0.18em]`.
- Use relaxed line heights and avoid dense text blocks.

## Layout and Spacing
- Mobile-first with elegant stacked layouts; desktop uses spacious two-column editorial compositions.
- Use generous section padding: `py-16`, `md:py-24`, `lg:py-28`.
- Product cards should have breathing room and soft shadow only on hover.
- Use thin hairline dividers: `border-velmora-line`.

## Components
- Primary buttons: solid gold with deep espresso text, high contrast, subtle hover to bronze.
- Secondary buttons: transparent or porcelain with thin gold/line border.
- Nav: transparent over hero, solid ivory/porcelain after scroll.
- Cart drawer: porcelain surface, explicit ink text, gold accents.

## Do's
- Keep imagery warm, close-up, tactile, and jewelry-focused.
- Use restrained transitions: opacity, translate, and shadow.
- Keep all labels and prices highly legible.

## Don'ts
- Do not use loud sale colors, heavy gradients, neon accents, or discount-style badges.
- Do not overcrowd product grids or make mobile cards too small.
- Do not place low-contrast taupe text on champagne or gold backgrounds without explicit readable color.
