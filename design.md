# ARTITECT MACHINERY Design System

## Brand feel
ARTITECT MACHINERY should feel elegant, precise, reliable, and approachable. The visual language blends premium industrial engineering with a user-friendly product showcase.

## Colors
- Primary surface: deep slate and near-black gradients for a premium machinery feel. Example Tailwind classes: `bg-slate-950`, `bg-slate-900`, `text-white`.
- Secondary surface: warm light gray for readable content sections. Example: `bg-stone-50`, `bg-white`, `text-slate-900`.
- Accent: amber/gold for calls to action and highlights, suggesting precision metalwork. Example: `bg-amber-500`, `text-amber-400`, `border-amber-300`.
- Supporting cool tone: steel blue/slate for industrial trust. Example: `text-slate-500`, `border-slate-200`, `bg-slate-100`.

## Typography
- Use Inter as the default font for clean technical readability.
- Headlines should be bold, tight, and confident: `font-bold tracking-tight`.
- Body text should be relaxed and readable: `leading-7 text-slate-600`.

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous section padding: `py-16`, `lg:py-24`.
- Keep content width controlled with `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-6 lg:px-8`.
- Use rounded cards and subtle shadows: `rounded-3xl shadow-xl shadow-slate-900/10`.
- Desktop layouts can use 2–3 columns; mobile should stack cleanly.

## Components
- Navigation: simple sticky header with clear calls to action.
- Hero: strong headline, concise value proposition, machinery-focused visual panel.
- Product cards: use clear product keywords, features, and inquiry actions.
- Trust section: show production quality, customization, and after-sales support.
- Contact section: simple, low-friction inquiry form styling.

## Do's
- Keep text contrast strong and explicit on every surface.
- Use amber accents sparingly for important actions and key figures.
- Use clean borders and spacing to make technical information easy to scan.

## Don'ts
- Do not use low-contrast gray text on dark backgrounds.
- Do not crowd product information on mobile.
- Do not use playful or overly decorative styling that weakens the engineering brand.
