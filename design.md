# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm ivory surfaces, deep espresso contrast, muted champagne accents, generous whitespace, thin hairline rules, and soft shadows. The store should feel premium, intimate, and giftable without discount-style urgency.

## Palette
- Deep base: espresso and soft black (`velmora-espresso`, `velmora-ink`) for high-contrast text, navigation, and premium surfaces.
- Main surfaces: warm ivory and pearl (`velmora-ivory`, `velmora-pearl`) for editorial breathing room.
- Accent: champagne gold (`velmora-champagne`, `velmora-gold`) for CTAs, icons, star ratings, and subtle rules.
- Supporting neutral: stone/taupe (`velmora-stone`, `velmora-taupe`) for cards, muted panels, and secondary text.

## Typography
- Headings and brand moments use an elegant serif: Cormorant Garamond.
- Body and interface text use Manrope.
- Product names are uppercase with wide letter-spacing.

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous vertical rhythm with mobile-first spacing and larger desktop sections.
- Prefer max-width editorial containers (`max-w-7xl`) with full-bleed hero and select image bands.
- Use thin hairline dividers (`border-velmora-espresso/10` or `border-white/15`).

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: either solid espresso/champagne or outlined hairline styles with subtle hover transitions.
- Product cards are calm and image-led with a hover second-image reveal and a discreet quick add action.
- Cart drawer and overlays should use ivory/espresso with explicit high-contrast foreground colors.

## Do
- Keep copy elegant and restrained.
- Use soft transitions and transforms, not loud animations.
- Use warm jewelry imagery through Strikingly image tags.

## Don't
- Do not use bright sale colors, aggressive discount styling, or generic marketplace layouts.
- Do not place low-contrast text on image or colored backgrounds without overlays.
- Do not introduce unrelated colors outside the defined palette.
