# Velmora Fine Jewelry Visual System

## Direction
Quiet luxury with a warm editorial point of view. The site should feel intimate, elevated, and premium-but-accessible rather than flashy or discount-driven.

## Palette
Use one consistent deep-warm editorial palette that flatters gold jewelry:
- Ink: deep espresso-black for primary backgrounds and text on light surfaces (`velmora-ink`, `#1f1915`).
- Cocoa: softened dark brown for panels and overlays (`velmora-cocoa`, `#3b2d25`).
- Pearl: warm ivory page background (`velmora-pearl`, `#f8f3eb`).
- Champagne: refined metallic accent for CTAs, borders, icons, and highlights (`velmora-champagne`, `#c79a55`).
- Linen: warm neutral cards and strips (`velmora-linen`, `#eee4d6`).
- Mist: pale neutral borders and subtle backgrounds (`velmora-mist`, `#ded2c2`).
- Rose: gentle blush for newsletter/accent panels (`velmora-rose`, `#d8b7a3`).

## Typography
- Headings and brand moments: elegant serif using Cormorant Garamond. Use generous line-height and refined letter spacing.
- Body and interface: Manrope or Inter-style sans-serif for clarity.
- Product names: uppercase with wide letter-spacing, serif, calm and editorial.

## Layout and Spacing
- Use generous whitespace, especially around product grids and editorial sections.
- Prefer thin hairline dividers, restrained borders, and soft shadows.
- Desktop layouts should feel editorial and spacious; mobile should remain concise and easy to shop.
- Hero imagery should be full-bleed and immersive with strong text contrast.

## Components
- Buttons: premium solid champagne on dark/ink surfaces; outlined ink/champagne variants on light surfaces. Rounded-full pills or refined rectangular CTAs only.
- Cards: warm ivory or linen surfaces with readable ink text, subtle border, and gentle hover lift.
- Navigation: transparent over hero, transitions to pearl background with ink text after scrolling.
- Cart drawer and overlays: explicit foreground/background color pairs for readability.

## Do
- Keep imagery warm, close-up, editorial, and jewelry-focused.
- Use gold/champagne accents sparingly.
- Ensure all text is highly readable against its surface.
- Use subtle transitions and avoid aggressive animation.

## Don't
- Do not use loud sale colors, neon tones, heavy gradients, or generic marketplace styling.
- Do not crowd product cards with badges or discount language.
- Do not use low-contrast muted text for important details.
