# SSourcing China Visual Design Guide

## Brand direction
Clean, trustworthy, practical B2B style for overseas buyers evaluating a China sourcing partner. The site should feel international, structured, and operational rather than promotional.

## Colors
- Primary navy: use `brand-navy` (`#0f2a43`) for headers, navigation, and high-trust surfaces.
- Action blue: use `brand-blue` (`#1d6fb8`) for primary CTAs, links, and active states.
- Deep teal: use `brand-teal` (`#0f766e`) for verification, quality, and process accents.
- Warm amber: use `brand-amber` (`#d97706`) sparingly for important highlights.
- Light background: use `brand-surface` (`#f5f8fb`) for page backgrounds.
- Soft border: use `brand-border` (`#d9e3ee`) for cards, forms, and separators.
- Text: use `brand-ink` (`#122033`) for body copy and `brand-muted` (`#5c6f82`) for supporting text.

## Typography
Use Inter from Google Fonts. Headings should be strong and clear with tight line-height. Body text should be practical and readable.

## Layout and spacing
Use wide desktop layouts with 2-column sections where appropriate. Keep generous padding, clear section separation, and consistent card grids. Mobile should stack cleanly with comfortable spacing.

## Components
- Cards: white background, subtle border, rounded corners, soft shadow.
- Buttons: filled blue for primary CTAs, white/navy bordered for secondary CTAs.
- Forms: explicit labels, readable placeholders, strong focus states, and clear success/error messages.
- Visuals: use realistic factory, inspection, warehouse, logistics, and product sourcing visuals through the stock image attribute system.

## Do
- Use specific, operational language.
- Make every CTA easy to find.
- Keep text contrast high on every background.
- Use practical trust points and process steps.

## Don't
- Do not use exaggerated claims such as guaranteed lowest price or risk-free sourcing.
- Do not use playful consumer styling.
- Do not hide important text in low-contrast muted colors.
