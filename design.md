# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand Direction
Velmora Fine Jewelry uses a quiet-luxury editorial style. The storefront should feel warm, refined, intimate, and premium without looking flashy or discount-driven.

## Visual Mood
- Warm editorial photography
- Soft contrast and generous whitespace
- Thin dividers and delicate borders
- Gold-friendly color surfaces
- Calm, elevated product presentation

## Color Palette
Use one consistent direction across the entire site.

- `ink`: deep espresso base for hero, footer, and high-contrast surfaces. Example Tailwind token usage: `bg-ink`, `text-ivory`
- `ivory`: warm off-white page background. Example: `bg-ivory`, `text-ink`
- `sand`: soft neutral card background. Example: `bg-sand`
- `bronze`: refined metallic accent for buttons, highlights, and emphasis. Example: `bg-bronze`, `text-ink`
- `rose`: muted blush accent for subtle editorial warmth. Example: `bg-rose`
- `line`: hairline border color. Example: `border-line`
- `muted`: secondary readable text color. Example: `text-muted`

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond
- Body, UI, labels: Inter
- Product names should be uppercase with generous tracking. Example: `uppercase tracking-[0.28em]`
- Large editorial headlines should use soft leading and balanced spacing.

## Spacing
- Use generous vertical rhythm.
- Prefer section padding around `py-16 md:py-24`
- Constrain content with centered containers around `max-w-7xl`
- Product cards and panels should breathe; avoid crowded layouts.

## Borders and Shadows
- Use thin borders with `border-line`
- Use subtle shadows only, never heavy card shadows
- Rounded corners should feel tailored, not overly soft

## Buttons
- Primary buttons: bronze fill with dark text or ivory text only if contrast remains strong
- Secondary buttons: transparent with line border
- Hover states should be subtle, smooth, and premium
- Avoid loud gradients and high-saturation CTAs

## Components
- Navigation should be transparent over the hero and become solid on scroll
- Trust bar should be slim and understated
- Product cards should emphasize imagery first, with minimal clutter
- Quick add interactions should feel smooth and restrained
- Filters and selectors should use pill and chip patterns
- Cart drawer should feel like a premium boutique panel

## Do
- Keep text readable with explicit foreground colors on every surface
- Maintain high image prominence and clean whitespace
- Use serif display type for emotional emphasis only
- Use muted supporting copy, but keep it accessible

## Don't
- Do not use neon, bright sale colors, or discount-style badges
- Do not mix multiple visual directions
- Do not create cluttered product cards or dense mobile layouts
- Do not rely on low-contrast text for elegance
