# SSourcing China Design System

## Brand direction
SSourcing China should feel dependable, practical, international, and B2B-first. The site should look like a sourcing partner for serious buyers, not a flashy consumer brand.

## Visual language
- Clean layouts with generous spacing
- Strong information hierarchy
- Calm corporate palette built from Tailwind slate, white, sky, and amber accents
- Realistic operations visuals for factories, quality checks, packaging, and shipping
- Rounded corners that feel modern but not playful
- Soft shadows and subtle borders instead of heavy gradients everywhere

## Typography
- Primary font: Inter
- Headings: `font-semibold` to `font-bold`
- Body: `text-sm md:text-base leading-7`
- Eyebrows and metadata: `uppercase tracking-[0.2em] text-xs`

## Color usage
Use Tailwind utility classes instead of custom hex values.

- Page background: `bg-slate-50`
- Main surfaces: `bg-white`
- Dark emphasis sections: `bg-slate-950`
- Primary text: `text-slate-900`
- Secondary text: `text-slate-600`
- Muted text: `text-slate-500`
- Borders: `border-slate-200`
- Primary brand actions: `bg-sky-700 text-white hover:bg-sky-800`
- Secondary action accents: `bg-white text-slate-900 border border-slate-300`
- Trust/accent markers: `text-amber-500`, `bg-amber-50`

## Layout rules
- Use a wide but controlled content width: `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-4 sm:px-6 lg:px-8`
- Standard section rhythm: `py-16 md:py-24`
- Cards: `rounded-2xl border border-slate-200 bg-white shadow-sm`
- Large feature panels: `rounded-3xl`
- Desktop layouts should feel multi-column where appropriate; do not collapse everything into a single narrow stack on large screens

## Components
- Primary buttons should be solid sky blue with clear hover states
- Secondary buttons should be outlined or white on dark sections
- Section headings should pair a small eyebrow with a concise headline and practical supporting copy
- Data and trust panels should use icons, short bullets, and visible labels
- Forms should have strong labels, clear borders, and readable placeholder text

## Imagery
- Use realistic industrial and logistics imagery only where it supports trust
- Prioritize scenes of supplier meetings, production floors, inspection tools, packaging, warehouse handling, and container shipping
- Avoid generic lifestyle photos, abstract tech graphics, and overly dramatic effects

## Do
- Keep every text element clearly readable on its background
- Write concise, practical copy focused on buyer outcomes
- Use subtle contrast and strong spacing to create a professional feel
- Highlight process clarity, verification, and control points

## Do not
- Do not use exaggerated claims or hype-driven language
- Do not use neon colors, glassmorphism, or playful illustrations
- Do not use low-contrast gray text on light cards
- Do not overload pages with dense paragraphs without visual breaks
