# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand mood
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, feminine, and assured. The visual language should flatter gold jewelry and never feel loud, discount-driven, or overly trendy.

## Color direction
Use one consistent palette across the site:
- Ink: deep espresso-charcoal for headings, overlays, and high-contrast UI
- Pearl: warm ivory for the main page background
- Mist: soft beige for section shifts and cards
- Gold: muted brushed gold for CTAs, accents, icons, and focus states
- Sand: warm taupe for borders, dividers, and subtle surfaces
- Cocoa: softened brown for secondary text

Recommended Tailwind tokens:
- `bg-velmora-ink` / `text-velmora-ink`
- `bg-velmora-pearl` / `text-velmora-pearl`
- `bg-velmora-mist`
- `bg-velmora-gold` / `text-velmora-gold`
- `border-velmora-sand`
- `text-velmora-cocoa`

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond
- Body, UI, labels, buttons: Inter
- Product names must be uppercase with generous letter-spacing.
- Serif headlines should feel editorial and spacious, not overly ornate.

## Layout and spacing
- Prefer generous whitespace and breathing room.
- Use thin hairline borders and restrained shadows.
- Sections should feel calm and premium, with clear hierarchy.
- Mobile should be clean and touch-friendly; desktop should open up into wider editorial layouts.

## Components
- Buttons: premium, simple, and confident. Prefer solid gold or refined outline styles.
- Cards: rounded but not playful; use soft shadows and subtle hover motion.
- Navigation: minimal, refined, and highly legible in both transparent and solid states.
- Inputs: understated, elegant, and high-contrast.

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit editorial jewelry imagery.
- Prefer close-up product and lifestyle shots with dark or neutral backdrops.
- Imagery should feel aspirational, feminine, and tactile.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and text readable on every surface.
- Use gold as an accent, not as the main background everywhere.
- Maintain consistent spacing, border treatment, and typography across pages.
- Favor subtle transitions over dramatic animation.

## Don’t
- Do not use bright sale colors, discount styling, or loud gradients.
- Do not mix multiple visual directions.
- Do not use low-contrast beige-on-beige text.
- Do not make the storefront feel generic or template-like.
