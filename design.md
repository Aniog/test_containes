# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora uses a warm editorial palette inspired by velvet-lined jewelry boxes, candlelit interiors, and softly polished gold. The experience should feel premium, calm, and quietly confident.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`, elegant, high contrast, generous line height.
- Body and UI: `Inter`, clean and restrained.
- Product names: uppercase with wide letter spacing.

## Color palette
Add these as named Tailwind colors and use them consistently.
- `velvet`: `#211b18` — deep base for text, overlays, and premium surfaces.
- `espresso`: `#4d4038` — secondary dark tone.
- `truffle`: `#7b675b` — muted body copy and dividers.
- `champagne`: `#b8925e` — metallic accent, buttons, focus states.
- `rosegold`: `#d2b08d` — softer accent for gradients and supporting details.
- `porcelain`: `#f6f1eb` — site background.
- `bone`: `#ede4da` — cards, borders, and subtle fills.
- `ivory`: `#fffdf9` — elevated surfaces.

## Surfaces and borders
- Backgrounds should be light, warm, and airy.
- Use hairline borders in `bone` or low-opacity `truffle`.
- Elevated cards use `ivory` with very soft shadows.
- Dark editorial sections use `velvet` with `porcelain` text.

## Components
- Buttons: solid `champagne` or outlined `velvet`; rounded-full; never loud.
- Dividers: thin and understated.
- Inputs: warm ivory background, explicit `velvet` text, visible placeholder contrast.
- Product cards: image-first, lots of whitespace, subtle hover lift, quick add appears smoothly.

## Spacing
- Prefer generous vertical spacing: `py-16`, `py-20`, `py-24`.
- Max content width: `max-w-7xl`.
- Use breathing room around editorial text blocks and imagery.

## Shadows
- Use soft, diffuse shadows only.
- Avoid heavy or dark shadows.

## Do
- Keep the layout elegant and uncluttered.
- Maintain strong text contrast on every surface.
- Use accent color sparingly to make CTAs and metadata feel premium.

## Don’t
- Don’t use discount-store colors, oversized badges, or noisy gradients.
- Don’t mix multiple visual directions.
- Don’t allow text to inherit low-contrast colors on cards, overlays, or form fields.
