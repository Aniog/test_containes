# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora Fine Jewelry should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and premium without feeling flashy or inaccessible.

## Palette
Use one consistent palette across the whole storefront:
- Background: warm ivory / soft stone (`bg-velmora-ivory`, `bg-velmora-stone`)
- Main surface: deep espresso (`bg-velmora-espresso`)
- Accent metallic: champagne gold (`bg-velmora-champagne`, `text-velmora-champagne`)
- Supporting neutral: taupe (`bg-velmora-taupe`, `text-velmora-taupe`)
- Main text on light surfaces: `text-velmora-ink`
- Main text on dark surfaces: `text-velmora-ivory`
- Muted text: `text-velmora-muted`
- Divider: `border-velmora-line`

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`, elegant serif
- Body and UI: `Inter`, clean sans-serif
- Product names should be uppercase with tracking such as `tracking-[0.35em]`
- Editorial headlines should use generous leading and lighter serif weights

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous white space with large section padding, for example `py-16 md:py-24`
- Keep content width restrained using containers like `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-5 md:px-8`
- Use thin dividers with `border border-velmora-line/70`
- Cards should feel airy with soft shadows, subtle rounded corners, and clean image ratios

## Buttons and interactions
- Primary button: dark espresso background with champagne foreground, subtle hover lift
- Secondary button: transparent or ivory with espresso border and text
- Hover effects should be subtle: soft translate, opacity fade, or image crossfade
- Avoid loud gradients, oversized shadows, and sales-style badges

## Imagery
- Use warm editorial close-ups of gold jewelry on models or still-life surfaces
- Prefer dark neutral, stone, or skin-tone-friendly backgrounds that flatter gold tones
- Use large full-bleed or portrait images sparingly and intentionally

## Do
- Keep surfaces readable with explicit foreground colors
- Use refined serif headings and wide-tracked product names
- Maintain consistency in spacing, borders, and button treatments
- Make mobile layouts feel luxurious, not cramped

## Don't
- Do not use bright discount colors, harsh black/white contrast, or neon accents
- Do not use dense card grids with cramped text
- Do not mix multiple visual directions on different pages
- Do not leave text colors implicit on tinted or dark backgrounds
