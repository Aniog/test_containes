# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury with a warm editorial softness. The site must look premium, modern, and intentionally restrained.

## Palette
Use this palette consistently across the entire site.
- `velmora-ivory` `#F7F1EA` — primary page background
- `velmora-pearl` `#FBF7F2` — elevated cards and soft panels
- `velmora-sand` `#D9CCBD` — dividers, borders, subtle surfaces
- `velmora-bronze` `#AE8A67` — refined accent for buttons and highlights
- `velmora-espresso` `#1E1816` — primary dark text and hero surface
- `velmora-umber` `#5C4D43` — supporting text
- `velmora-mist` `#E8DDD2` — muted background fill behind imagery

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`, serif
- Body, interface, navigation: `Manrope`, sans-serif
- Product names should be uppercase with wide tracking

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous whitespace and editorial spacing
- Use thin borders and soft shadows instead of heavy cards
- Desktop layouts should feel spacious; mobile should feel polished and uncluttered

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: bronze solid buttons or espresso outlined buttons
- Cards should use warm light surfaces with clear readable text
- Hover states should be subtle, smooth, and never flashy
- Trust signals and meta details should use quiet small caps styling

## Imagery
- Favor warm-lit gold jewelry imagery on neutral or dark refined backgrounds
- Editorial portraits should feel intimate and elevated, never stock-photo generic
- UGC cards should mimic polished social reels with soft overlays and readable serif captions

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and all text explicitly readable
- Use bronze sparingly as an accent
- Preserve the calm editorial tone on every page

## Do not
- Do not use loud sale styling, bright discount colors, or cluttered badges
- Do not mix multiple visual directions
- Do not rely on inherited text colors on custom surfaces
