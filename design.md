# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm ivory surfaces, deep espresso/navy contrast, soft champagne metallic accents, generous whitespace, and restrained motion. The goal is premium demi-fine jewelry that feels intimate, polished, and accessible without looking discount-driven.

## Palette
- Base ink: `velmora-ink` (`#16110d`) for primary text and dark surfaces.
- Espresso: `velmora-espresso` (`#2a1f1a`) for elevated dark panels and footer.
- Ivory: `velmora-ivory` (`#fbf7ef`) for main page backgrounds.
- Parchment: `velmora-parchment` (`#f1e7d8`) for soft section blocks.
- Sand: `velmora-sand` (`#d9c6ad`) for hairline borders and subdued fills.
- Champagne: `velmora-champagne` (`#b9915b`) for premium CTAs and accents.
- Bronze: `velmora-bronze` (`#7a5633`) for hover states and supporting labels.
- Blush: `velmora-blush` (`#efe1d8`) for warm newsletter and testimonial accents.

## Typography
- Headings and product names use Cormorant Garamond, elegant serif with high contrast.
- Body and UI use Manrope, clean sans-serif.
- Product names are uppercase with wide tracking: `uppercase tracking-[0.22em]`.
- Editorial headings should use generous line-height and large type: `font-serif text-5xl md:text-7xl leading-none`.

## Layout & Spacing
- Use mobile-first layouts, then expand to editorial two-column and multi-column grids at `md:` and `lg:` breakpoints.
- Prefer spacious sections: `py-16 md:py-24`, containers `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-5 sm:px-8`.
- Use thin hairline dividers: `border border-velmora-sand/60` or `border-t border-velmora-sand/50`.
- Cards should have quiet shadows and soft radii: `rounded-[1.75rem] shadow-[0_24px_80px_rgba(42,31,26,0.08)]`.

## Buttons & Interactions
- Primary CTA: champagne fill with ink text, subtle bronze hover.
- Secondary CTA: transparent with champagne/ink hairline border.
- Transitions should be soft: `transition-all duration-500 ease-out`.
- Hover states reveal detail but stay restrained; no loud colors or heavy effects.

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit jewelry imagery on models, dark velvet, parchment, stone, or neutral backgrounds.
- Full-bleed hero and vertical reel cards should feel editorial and tactile.
- Avoid cold white-background catalog imagery as the dominant mood.

## Do's
- Keep strong contrast on every surface.
- Use serif headlines and product names consistently.
- Leave breathing room around products and editorial content.
- Use gold/champagne accents sparingly for premium emphasis.

## Don'ts
- Do not use discount badges, loud reds, neon colors, or cluttered sale language.
- Do not mix multiple visual directions.
- Do not rely on low-contrast beige text on ivory backgrounds.
- Do not overcrowd mobile cards or navigation.
