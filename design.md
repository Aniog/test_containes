# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm ivory surfaces, deep espresso text, restrained champagne-gold accents, generous whitespace, and soft photographic moments that flatter demi-fine gold jewelry.

## Palette
- Primary surface: warm ivory (`bg-velmora-ivory`) and pearl (`bg-velmora-pearl`).
- Deep base and text: ink (`text-velmora-ink`) and espresso (`bg-velmora-espresso`, `text-velmora-espresso`).
- Metallic accent: champagne (`bg-velmora-champagne`, `text-velmora-champagne`) and antique gold (`text-velmora-antique`).
- Supporting neutrals: sand, blush, smoke for borders, muted copy, and editorial blocks.

## Typography
- Headings and product names use Cormorant Garamond for an elegant editorial feel.
- Body and UI use Inter for clean readability.
- Product names are uppercase with wide letter spacing (`tracking-luxury`).

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous vertical rhythm: spacious sections (`py-16`, `md:py-24`) and comfortable max-width containers.
- Use thin hairline dividers (`border-velmora-sand/60`) rather than heavy borders.
- Desktop layouts should feel editorial and balanced; mobile layouts stack naturally with strong spacing.

## Components
- Buttons: premium solid espresso or champagne with subtle hover transitions; outlined buttons use hairline borders.
- Cards: soft shadows, warm ivory/pearl surfaces, clear foreground colors, and restrained hover movement.
- Imagery: large warm gold-jewelry imagery on neutral or deep backgrounds; no loud colors or discount styling.

## Do's
- Keep contrast strong and text explicitly readable.
- Use quiet, warm, premium language.
- Let imagery and whitespace carry the luxury feel.

## Don'ts
- Do not use neon colors, heavy gradients, sale-heavy badges, or cluttered layouts.
- Do not use low-contrast beige-on-beige text.
- Do not introduce arbitrary hex colors in component class names; add named colors to Tailwind when needed.
