# ARTITECT MACHINERY Design System

## Brand direction
ARTITECT MACHINERY should feel elegant, precise, industrial, and trustworthy. The site should present heavy machinery with a premium tone while staying friendly and easy to scan for first-time buyers.

## Visual style
- Use a refined industrial palette built from Tailwind color tokens.
- Primary surfaces: `bg-slate-950`, `bg-slate-900`, `bg-stone-100`, `bg-white`
- Accent color: `amber-500`, `amber-400`, `amber-300`
- Support tones: `slate-700`, `slate-600`, `slate-400`, `stone-500`
- Borders: `border-white/10`, `border-slate-200`, `border-amber-400/20`
- Shadows: `shadow-xl`, `shadow-2xl`, `shadow-slate-950/10`

## Typography
- Use Inter for body copy and navigation.
- Use a serif accent style only for small premium moments if needed, but keep most text modern and crisp.
- Headings should feel confident and engineered: `font-semibold` to `font-bold`.
- Body copy should remain highly readable with `text-base` or `text-lg` and comfortable line height.

## Layout and spacing
- Use wide, clean sections with generous vertical spacing: `py-16`, `py-20`, `py-24`.
- Keep content inside centered containers such as `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-6 lg:px-8`.
- Use clear card groupings with rounded corners: `rounded-3xl`, `rounded-2xl`.
- Desktop layouts should feel structured with 2 to 4 columns when space allows.
- Mobile layouts should stack cleanly with strong spacing and no cramped text.

## Components
- Hero section should combine strong headline copy, trust signals, and a prominent CTA.
- Product cards should use subtle borders, soft gradients, and concise specification summaries.
- Metrics and process blocks should be simple, readable, and confidence-building.
- CTA sections should stand out using dark background with warm accent buttons.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and readable on every surface.
- Use premium but restrained color usage.
- Prefer simple icon-supported cards for features and product highlights.
- Keep copy direct, buyer-friendly, and professional.

## Don't
- Do not use neon colors or playful styling.
- Do not overcrowd sections with too many decorative elements.
- Do not use low-contrast gray text on light cards.
- Do not rely on dense technical jargon without context.
