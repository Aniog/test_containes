# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and premium without ever looking flashy or sales-driven. The mood is intimate and polished, with generous whitespace, elegant typography, and restrained accents that flatter gold jewelry.

## Visual rules
- Use a deep espresso foundation for high-contrast surfaces and hero overlays.
- Use warm parchment and soft sand surfaces for the primary page background and cards.
- Use brushed gold only as an accent for buttons, dividers, badges, and highlights.
- Keep layouts spacious with comfortable vertical rhythm and thin hairline separators.
- Prefer soft rounded corners, subtle shadows, and calm transitions.
- Product names should always be uppercase with wide tracking.

## Typography
- Display / editorial typography: Cormorant Garamond via `.font-display`
- UI / body typography: Inter via default sans styles
- Product labels: `.font-display` + `.tracking-luxury` + uppercase

## Core utility classes
- Backgrounds: `.bg-velmora-parchment`, `.bg-velmora-sand`, `.bg-velmora-ink`
- Text: `.text-velmora-ink`, `.text-velmora-muted`, `.text-velmora-gold`
- Borders: `.border-velmora-line`
- Typography: `.font-display`, `.tracking-luxury`
- Shadows: `.shadow-velmora`, `.shadow-velmora-soft`

## Component guidance
- Buttons should feel premium: solid espresso or brushed-gold accent, or a refined outlined style.
- Hero sections should use cinematic imagery, layered gradients, and left-aligned editorial copy.
- Product cards should feel airy and elevated, not compressed or discount-like.
- Filters, drawers, and navigation should use light blur, soft borders, and clear readable text.

## Do
- Keep all text highly readable against every surface.
- Use subtle hover transitions and calm motion.
- Let photography, spacing, and typography carry the luxury feel.
- Maintain a warm tonal palette across all pages.

## Don’t
- Do not use bright promotional colors, loud gradients, or bargain-style badges.
- Do not crowd cards or stack desktop layouts into overly narrow columns.
- Do not use heavy borders or dense UI chrome.
- Do not mix multiple visual directions.
