# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: editorial, warm, refined, and premium without feeling cold or inaccessible. The overall mood is intimate and polished, with imagery that flatters gold jewelry and typography that feels elevated.

## Palette
Use one consistent palette sitewide.

- Deep espresso background: `bg-stone-950`
- Soft editorial ivory: `bg-stone-100` / `bg-stone-50`
- Warm champagne gold accent: `text-amber-300`, `border-amber-300/40`, `bg-amber-300`
- Muted stone surfaces: `bg-stone-900`, `bg-stone-800/70`, `bg-stone-200`
- Primary text on dark: `text-stone-50`
- Primary text on light: `text-stone-900`
- Secondary text: `text-stone-300` on dark, `text-stone-600` on light

Do not introduce bright promotional colors, discount red, or generic black-and-white minimalism without warmth.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: `Cormorant Garamond`, elegant, high contrast, generous line height
- Body and interface: `Inter`
- Product names should be uppercase with expanded tracking, for example `tracking-[0.28em]`

## Layout and spacing
- Generous whitespace with wide horizontal padding: `px-5 md:px-8 lg:px-12 xl:px-16`
- Large editorial sections with vertical breathing room: `py-16 md:py-24`
- Thin dividers: `border-stone-200/15` on dark or `border-stone-300/70` on light
- Use rounded corners sparingly; prefer subtle radii like `rounded-2xl` or `rounded-3xl`

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: solid champagne-gold on dark backgrounds, dark outlined or dark solid on light backgrounds
- Cards should use subtle shadows such as `shadow-[0_20px_60px_rgba(0,0,0,0.18)]`
- Hover transitions should be restrained: `transition-all duration-500 ease-out`
- Inputs should feel clean and tactile, with clear borders and readable placeholders

## Imagery
- Prefer warm-lit, close-up jewelry imagery with skin tones, neck/ear detail, and soft shadows
- Backgrounds behind products should be dark espresso, warm taupe, stone, or champagne neutral
- UGC/reel cards should be vertical and fashion-forward

## Do
- Keep text highly readable against every surface
- Use serif headlines to create an editorial feeling
- Let whitespace and photography carry the luxury tone
- Use subtle metallic accents, not shiny gradients

## Don’t
- Don’t use loud sale banners or bright ecommerce colors
- Don’t crowd desktop layouts into mobile-style single columns everywhere
- Don’t leave important text colors implicit on custom backgrounds
- Don’t make buttons or cards look generic or discount-driven
