# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury for demi-fine jewelry: warm, editorial, elevated, and intimate. The storefront should never feel promotional, crowded, flashy, or discount-driven.

## Color system
Use one cohesive palette across the entire site.

- Canvas: `#F7F2EA` — soft editorial ivory for the main page background
- Surface: `#FBF7F1` — slightly lighter card and panel background
- Panel: `#EFE5D8` — warm secondary section background
- Border: `#D8CAB8` — thin hairline dividers and subtle outlines
- Ink: `#251B16` — primary text and headings
- Truffle: `#4E3D34` — secondary dark text
- Muted: `#7B6A5D` — supporting copy and metadata
- Accent: `#B89465` — refined metallic gold accent for buttons and highlights
- Accent Deep: `#8E6A3A` — hover and pressed accent state
- Accent Soft: `#E2D2BA` — soft accent backgrounds and pills
- Cream: `#FFFDF9` — light text on dark imagery
- Shadow: `#1E1510` — dark image overlay and footer base

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond
- Body, UI labels, and metadata: Inter
- Product names should be uppercase with generous letter-spacing.
- Headings should feel editorial and elegant, not ornate.

## Layout and spacing
- Use generous spacing and breathing room.
- Desktop layouts should feel balanced and editorial, not stacked like mobile.
- Prefer `max-w-7xl` containers with roomy side padding.
- Use thin borders, soft shadows, and restrained hover movement.

## Components
- Primary buttons: solid accent background with cream text, rounded-full shape.
- Secondary buttons: transparent or surface background with border in accent or border color.
- Cards: light surface background, subtle border, gentle shadow.
- Navigation: transparent over hero on home, then solid blurred surface on scroll.
- Footer: dark shadow background with cream text and subtle muted dividers.

## Imagery
- Imagery should feel warm-lit, close, tactile, and editorial.
- Favor jewelry on skin, mirror reflections, velvet boxes, stone trays, and styling details.
- Never use loud gradients, neon tones, or generic sales imagery.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and readable.
- Use uppercase micro-labels for categories and metadata.
- Let whitespace carry the luxury tone.
- Keep accent usage restrained and premium.

## Don’t
- Do not use discount badges, bright sale colors, or loud animations.
- Do not mix multiple visual directions.
- Do not leave important text colors implicit on custom backgrounds.
- Do not make the desktop layout feel like a stretched mobile page.
