# Design Guidelines - ARTITECT MACHINERY

## Visual Style: Elegant Industrial

The design should reflect the precision and robustness of industrial machinery while maintaining an elegant, premium feel. Think high-end engineering meets architectural clarity.

### Typography
- **Headings**: 'Inter', sans-serif (Bold, wide tracking for industrial feel) or a clean Serif if available. Let's stick to 'Inter' with varied weights for a modern, high-end engineering look.
- **Body**: 'Inter', sans-serif. Regular weight for readability.
- **Accents**: Monospace for technical specs or model numbers.

### Color Palette
- **Primary (Deep Industrial)**: `#1A1F2C` (Charcoal Navy) - `bg-slate-900`, `text-slate-900`
- **Secondary (Metallic)**: `#8E9196` (Slate Gray) - `text-slate-500`
- **Accent (Precision)**: `#0EA5E9` (Sky Blue) - `text-sky-500`, `bg-sky-500`
- **Background**: `#FFFFFF` (White) and `#F8FAFC` (Slate 50) for sectioning.

### Components
- **Buttons**: Rounded-md or none, slightly larger padding, subtle shadows.
- **Cards**: Minimal borders, subtle hover effects, clean typography.
- **Icons**: Thin-line icons (Lucide React) for a modern look.

### Spacing
- Generous whitespace to highlight product machinery details.
- Consistent padding: `px-6 py-12` for sections.

### Do's
- Use high-quality stock images of metalwork and machinery.
- Use technical grids and clean lines.
- Keep the navigation simple and sticky.

### Don'ts
- Use overly bright or neon colors (except for small interaction states).
- Use rounded, bubbly UI elements.
- Clutter the page with too much text.
