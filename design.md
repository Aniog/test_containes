# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora Fine Jewelry uses a quiet-luxury editorial aesthetic: warm, refined, feminine, premium, and minimal. The brand should feel elevated and giftable without looking flashy, discount-driven, or mass-market.

## Color palette
Commit to one cohesive direction sitewide:
- Background base: deep espresso plum for immersive hero and premium framing. Example Tailwind intent: `bg-stone-950` mixed with custom `bg-brand-ink`.
- Surface: warm ivory and soft pearl neutrals for content sections. Example: `bg-stone-50`, `bg-neutral-100`.
- Text primary: dark espresso on light surfaces, warm ivory on dark surfaces. Example: `text-stone-900`, `text-stone-100`.
- Accent metallic: muted champagne gold. Use sparingly for buttons, outlines, icons, star ratings, and dividers. Example named color intent: `brand-gold`.
- Secondary accent: dusty rose taupe for subtle highlights only. Example named color intent: `brand-rose`.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: elegant serif such as Cormorant Garamond.
- Body/UI: Inter.
- Product names should be uppercase with generous letter-spacing.

## Layout and spacing
- Generous whitespace, large imagery, clean grid structure.
- Use thin hairline dividers and understated borders.
- Desktop should feel editorial, not stacked like mobile.
- Mobile should remain airy with smaller but still premium spacing.

## Components
- Buttons: premium, calm, slightly rounded, no playful pill look. Prefer `rounded-full` only for tiny icon chips; main CTAs should be `rounded-none` or softly rounded.
- Cards: minimal borders, soft shadows, clear readable text, restrained hover movement.
- Navigation: transparent over hero, solid after scroll.
- Cart drawer: refined panel with clear hierarchy and strong readable text.

## Motion
- Use subtle transitions only: fade, slight translate, gentle scale on image hover.
- Avoid bouncy or exaggerated animations.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and explicit on every surface.
- Use serif headlines to create an editorial luxury feel.
- Keep product imagery warm, elevated, and giftable.
- Use gold accents sparingly.

## Don’t
- Do not use loud promo colors, discount badges, or heavy gradients.
- Do not use low-contrast text on beige or dark panels.
- Do not overcrowd sections or reduce whitespace too much.
- Do not mix multiple visual directions on the same site.
