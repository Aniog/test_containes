# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, refined, and intimate. The experience should flatter gold jewelry and feel premium without becoming dark, heavy, or flashy.

## Palette
Use one consistent, elegant direction across the entire storefront:
- `canvas` — soft porcelain beige for page backgrounds
- `pearl` — warm off-white for cards and elevated surfaces
- `mist` — soft neutral for subtle section contrast
- `sand` — muted champagne for accents and fills
- `gold` — refined metallic accent for premium emphasis
- `bronze` — deeper accent tone for hover and emphasis
- `ink` — primary text color
- `bark` — dark surface color for hero overlays and footer
- `line` — hairline borders and dividers
- `muted` — secondary copy

## Typography
- Headings, product names, and logo: `font-serif` using Cormorant Garamond
- Body, buttons, labels, UI, and forms: `font-sans` using Inter
- Product names should be uppercase with wide tracking using `tracking-luxe`

## Components and styling
- Buttons: solid gold for primary CTA, ink or outlined neutral for secondary actions
- Cards: warm light surfaces, thin borders, soft shadows, rounded corners
- Dividers: hairline borders using `border-line`
- Imagery: large, editorial, warm-lit, and spacious
- Hover: subtle translate, opacity, and shadow transitions only
- Layout: generous whitespace, especially around hero, story, and product grids

## Recommended Tailwind tokens
- Backgrounds: `bg-canvas`, `bg-pearl`, `bg-mist`, `bg-bark`
- Text: `text-ink`, `text-muted`, `text-pearl`, `text-gold`
- Borders: `border-line`
- Buttons: `bg-gold text-pearl hover:bg-bronze`
- Serif titles: `font-serif tracking-luxe uppercase`

## Do
- Keep surfaces readable with explicit text colors
- Use warm contrast and restrained metallic accents
- Let images, type, and spacing communicate the premium feel
- Prioritize mobile clarity while keeping desktop layouts editorial

## Don't
- Do not use loud sale styling or bright discount colors
- Do not overcrowd cards or sections
- Do not use low-contrast text on beige or gold surfaces
- Do not mix multiple visual directions or accent palettes
