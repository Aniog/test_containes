# Design Document: MicroCosmos

## Visual Style
The "MicroCosmos" website should feel immersive, detailed, and awe-inspiring, mirroring the hidden beauty of the microscopic world. It combines scientific precision with artistic elegance.

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Inter, SemiBold/Bold (`font-sans font-semibold` or `font-bold`). Clean and modern.
- **Body Text:** Inter, Regular (`font-sans`). Highly legible at various sizes.
- **Accents:** Small caps or tracking for labels (`tracking-wider uppercase text-xs`).

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep Midnight Blue (`bg-slate-950`, `text-slate-50`).
- **Secondary:** Bioluminescent Teal/Cyan (`text-teal-400`, `bg-teal-400`).
- **Accent:** Deep Purple (`text-purple-500`) and Scientific White/Gray (`text-slate-300`).
- **Backgrounds:** Mostly dark backgrounds to make microscopic imagery pop.

### Components
- **Cards:** Subtle borders or glassmorphism effects (`bg-slate-900/50 backdrop-blur-sm border border-slate-800`).
- **Buttons:** Sharp or slightly rounded (`rounded-md`), using high-contrast colors like teal against dark backgrounds.
- **Layout:** Spacious with generous padding (`py-20`, `px-6`) to allow imagery to breathe.

### Imagery
- **Style:** High-resolution, detailed microscopic photography.
- **Types:** Electron microscope images, colorful light microscopy, macro photography of miniatures.

## Do's and Don'ts
### Do's
- Use high-contrast between text and background.
- Incorporate smooth animations or transitions (e.g., hover effects on cards).
- Use large, high-quality images as backgrounds or focal points.
- Keep the navigation simple and unobtrusive.

### Don'ts
- Use overly bright or neon backgrounds that distract from the microscopic images.
- Overcrowd the layout with too much text.
- Use low-quality or pixelated images.
- Rely on default browser styles for buttons or forms.
