# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora Fine Jewelry uses a quiet-luxury editorial style. The mood is warm, refined, and intimate. The storefront should feel premium and modern without looking trendy, loud, discount-led, or mass-market.

## Color palette
Use one consistent dark editorial palette that flatters gold jewelry.

- Background: obsidian espresso (`bg-velmora-obsidian`) for hero, footer, drawers
- Surface: warm ivory (`bg-velmora-ivory`) for main page backgrounds
- Secondary surface: porcelain (`bg-velmora-porcelain`) for cards and sections
- Text primary: ink (`text-velmora-ink`) on light surfaces
- Text inverse: ivory (`text-velmora-ivory`) on dark surfaces
- Accent: antique gold (`bg-velmora-gold`, `text-velmora-gold`) for buttons, dividers, ratings, highlights
- Muted text: taupe (`text-velmora-taupe`) for supporting copy
- Borders: mist (`border-velmora-mist`) as thin hairline dividers

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond, elegant and high-contrast
- Body and UI: Inter
- Product names: uppercase with wide tracking such as `tracking-[0.28em]` when needed in JSX, or nearest tokenized utility where possible
- Headings should have airy line-height and generous spacing

## Spacing and layout
- Generous whitespace, especially around hero, story, and newsletter sections
- Keep content width restrained with centered containers
- Prefer 1px dividers and soft shadows over heavy borders
- Desktop layouts should stay editorial and multi-column; do not collapse into mobile-style stacks on large screens

## Components
- Buttons feel premium: either solid antique gold with dark text or transparent with fine border
- Cards should use warm surfaces and subtle shadows
- Hover states should be restrained: slight lift, opacity shift, image swap, or divider accent
- Sticky navigation begins transparent over hero and becomes solid dark on scroll
- Cart drawer should feel like a boutique tray: dark shell, readable totals, clear actions

## Imagery
- Use warm-lit jewelry imagery on neutral or dark backgrounds
- Prefer close-up editorial portraits, ear styling, necklace crops, gift details
- Avoid generic white-background marketplace imagery

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and text always readable
- Use serif headlines to create a luxury tone
- Maintain consistency in gold accents and muted supporting copy
- Use thin dividers and subtle motion

## Don't
- Do not use bright sale colors, badges, or discount banners
- Do not mix multiple palette directions
- Do not use oversized rounded playful UI
- Do not rely on low-contrast beige-on-beige text
