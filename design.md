# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Brand direction
Velmora should feel like quiet luxury: warm, editorial, feminine, and refined. The interface should feel premium but still approachable for a direct-to-consumer jewelry shopper.

## Palette
Use one consistent warm-editorial palette that flatters gold jewelry.

- Ink: deep espresso for text and high-contrast surfaces. Example Tailwind class: `text-ink`, `bg-ink`
- Cocoa: softened dark neutral for secondary dark panels. Example: `bg-cocoa`
- Pearl: the main page background, soft ivory with warmth. Example: `bg-pearl`
- Sand: dividers, borders, muted panels. Example: `border-sand`, `bg-sand`
- Gold: restrained accent for buttons, highlights, ratings, and premium moments. Example: `bg-gold`, `text-gold`
- Gold Deep: hover and pressed accent state. Example: `bg-gold-deep`
- Ivory: bright surface for cards and overlays. Example: `bg-ivory`

## Typography
- Headings and product names: elegant serif using Cormorant Garamond
- Body, navigation, labels, and UI: Inter
- Product names should be uppercase with generous tracking
- Editorial headings should be large, airy, and never cramped

## Layout and spacing
- Mobile-first, but desktop should feel expansive and editorial
- Use generous vertical spacing between sections
- Prefer thin dividers over heavy borders
- Cards should have soft shadows and refined edges
- Keep content aligned to a consistent centered container

## Components
- Buttons should feel premium: solid gold for primary, transparent or ivory outlined for secondary
- Sticky header should begin transparent over the homepage hero and become solid on scroll
- Product cards should feel elevated but restrained, never discount-store styled
- Use subtle hover transitions only; avoid bouncy or flashy motion

## Imagery
- Prioritize close-up gold jewelry imagery with warm light, neutral or dark backdrops, and editorial styling
- Lifestyle imagery should feel polished, intimate, and aspirational
- UGC/reel imagery should still feel elevated and cohesive with the brand tone

## Do
- Use strong contrast for all text
- Keep the interface calm, spacious, and premium
- Use thin separators and warm surfaces
- Let imagery and typography carry the luxury feel

## Don’t
- Do not use bright sale colors, loud gradients, or discount-store styling
- Do not crowd cards or sections with dense UI
- Do not use low-contrast text on decorative backgrounds
- Do not mix multiple visual directions in one page
