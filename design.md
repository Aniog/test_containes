# Velmora Fine Jewelry Design System

## Direction
Velmora uses a quiet-luxury editorial direction: warm ivory surfaces, deep espresso text, champagne-gold accents, refined serif headings, generous whitespace, hairline borders, and soft shadows. The store should feel premium and calm, never loud, discounted, or generic.

## Palette
- Page background: warm ivory (`bg-[#f8f1e8]` only through theme tokens/utilities, not scattered arbitrary values)
- Deep base: espresso/black-brown for high-contrast text and dark editorial sections
- Card surface: pearl / soft cream
- Accent: champagne gold for CTAs, dividers, pills, and small details
- Muted text: warm taupe for secondary copy
- Borders: low-contrast champagne hairlines

Use strong contrast on every surface: dark text on ivory/pearl, ivory text on espresso, and espresso text on gold buttons.

## Typography
- Headings and product names: Cormorant Garamond, elegant serif
- Body and UI: Manrope, clean sans-serif
- Product names: uppercase, wide letter spacing
- Use large, airy headlines and restrained body sizes.

## Layout & Spacing
- Mobile-first with comfortable spacing and no crowded grids.
- Desktop uses editorial two-column and multi-column compositions.
- Use generous vertical padding, max-width containers, and thin dividers.
- Product cards should have calm whitespace and image-forward layouts.

## Components
- Buttons: premium pill or slim rectangular forms, champagne filled or espresso outlined.
- Cards: pearl/ivory surfaces, hairline borders, subtle hover lift, soft shadow.
- Navigation: transparent over hero, solid ivory on scroll.
- Cart drawer: espresso text on ivory, clear quantities and totals.

## Do's
- Use serif headlines and subtle italic editorial details.
- Keep transitions soft: opacity, transform, border color.
- Use stock image tags for jewelry/model imagery and warm editorial contexts.
- Keep labels and UI copy legible on all backgrounds.

## Don'ts
- Do not use bright sale colors, generic blue links, heavy shadows, or loud gradients.
- Do not stack desktop layouts into mobile-only patterns at large screen sizes.
- Do not use low-contrast gold text for important copy.
