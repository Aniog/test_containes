# Velmora Fine Jewelry Visual Design

## Direction
Quiet luxury with a warm editorial point of view. The site should feel intimate, refined, and premium-but-accessible rather than promotional or trend-driven.

## Color Palette
- Ink: deep espresso-plum for premium contrast and hero overlays. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-ink`, `text-velmora-ink`.
- Porcelain: warm ivory page surface. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-porcelain`.
- Champagne: soft neutral cards and sections. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-champagne`.
- Gold: restrained metallic accent for buttons, borders, stars, and small labels. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-gold`, `text-velmora-gold`.
- Cocoa: secondary text and subtle UI. Tailwind example: `text-velmora-cocoa`.
- Blush: gentle editorial accent for newsletter and small surfaces. Tailwind example: `bg-velmora-blush`.

## Typography
- Headings and product names use an elegant serif: Cormorant Garamond.
- Body and UI use a clean sans-serif: Manrope.
- Product names are uppercase with wide tracking.
- Use generous line-height and whitespace; avoid cramped merchandising layouts.

## Components
- Buttons: refined, minimal, either solid gold with ink text or outlined ink/gold. Use subtle hover movement and soft transitions.
- Cards: warm ivory/champagne surfaces, hairline borders, restrained shadows.
- Dividers: thin hairline borders in low-opacity cocoa/gold.
- Navigation: transparent over hero and solid ivory on scroll.
- Imagery: warm gold jewelry on skin, dark stone, silk, cream, and editorial neutral backgrounds.

## Do
- Keep contrast strong and text readable.
- Use large editorial images and asymmetric whitespace.
- Keep animations subtle and premium.
- Use rounded corners sparingly; prefer soft large radii over playful shapes.

## Don't
- Do not use discount-style red labels, loud gradients, or dense product badges.
- Do not mix many accent colors.
- Do not rely on low-contrast beige text for important labels or prices.
